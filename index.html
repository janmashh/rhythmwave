Frequently Asked Questions

These are frequently asked questions regarding our topic:

How does our brain receive or take the music that comes in?

Ever wonder how we feel giddy and jumpy when we start listening to music? That’s because the music entered into our brain reaches the pleasure center which boosts the dopamine. Dopamine is the hormone that gives us the sense of pleasure to our brain.

What type of music is best for the mind and body?

Coming from researchers’ studies, music you enjoy can improve one’s cognitive function because of the repetitive patterns the brain makes when listening to that kind of music.

Are there any specific music genres or components that are known to have particular effects on mental health?

According to the studies of researchers, everyone reacts differently to music. Some people enjoy peaceful calming music, while others prefer energetic, fast-paced music. Some people enjoy listening to rap music, while others prefer instrumentals. However, all of these genres have the same effect on the human brain.

What research has been done to support the therapeutic advantages of music such as stress reduction or cognitive enhancement?

Though because music therapy may be highly individualized, it is also appropriate for people of all ages—not just adults but also young children could get benefits just by listening to music or the songs they like. It's also adaptable, offering benefits for those with varying degrees of musical expertise and mental or physical health difficulties.
